REGISTRATION POLICY FOR IEEE SILCON-2023

-In order to publish your paper in the IEEE SILCON-2023 Conference Proceedings and to be eligible for publication in IEEE Xplore, at least one author of an accepted paper is required to register for the conference.

-The paper must be presented by an author of that paper at the conference unless the TPC Chair grants permission in advance of the event for a substitute presenter who is qualified both to present and answer questions.

-Non-refundable registration fees must be paid prior to uploading the final IEEE formatted, publication-ready version of the paper.

-Receipt of the Registration Fee will be provided to the registered authors only.

The registration of paper for presentation at IEEE SILCON-2023 is a two-step process as described below:

Step 1: PAYMENT OF REGISTRATION FEE (Deadline: 10 October 2023)

Step 2: PREPARATION AND SUBMISSION OF CAMERA-READY PAPER (Deadline: 10 October 2023)

Information for the PAYMENT OF REGISTRATION FEE (Step 1):

The authors are required to pay the non-refundable registration fee for IEEE SILCON-2023 to the Conference Bank Account with the following details:

Account Name: IEEE SILCON

Account Number: 41176303872

IFS Code: SBIN0007061

Bank Name: State Bank of India

Branch Name: NIT SILCHAR

MICR Number: 788002004

SWIFT Code: SBININBB481

The Indian resident authors can make the payment of the registration fee to the aforementioned bank account via any of the available domestic fund transfer modes, e.g., NEFT, UPI transfer, internet banking, mobile banking, etc. The foreign authors can make payment of the registration fee via international wire transfer to the aforementioned conference bank account.

After successful payment of the registration fee, the authors are required to fill details in the following google form: https://forms.gle/pU2kdy8TU8pznKVK8

The invoice/receipt of the authors' registration for IEEE SILCON-2023 will be generated after the successful verification of transaction details in the conference account.

Upon completion of Step 1, the authors are required to proceed with the submission of the final camera-ready version of the accepted manuscript. The detailed instructions for the preparation of the final camera-ready paper are given below:

PREPARATION AND SUBMISSION OF CAMERA-READY PAPERS (Step 2)

I. TAKE INTO ACCOUNT THE REVIEWERS' RECOMMENDATIONS IN PREPARING THE FINAL VERSION OF YOUR PAPER.

The final camera-ready version of your paper should account for all the reviewers’ feedback. All authors must agree to the final version. Check carefully that the authors’ names and affiliations are correct in the PDF file of your paper. Check also that the list of authors is correct in the EDAS platform, as we will use metadata from this platform to produce the author index of the conference proceedings.

II. CONFIRM THAT YOUR PAPER IS PROPERLY FORMATTED.

A. Papers must be written in English and formatted following the standard IEEE double-column conference template (US letter size) available from:

http://www.ieee.org/conferences_events/conferences/publishing/templates.html

B. Your paper should not include any header or footer information (e.g., page numbers) in the top/bottom margins. If your paper has been prepared using Microsoft Word, please ensure that you have used the most current version, which will help reduce word-to-pdf conversion issues such as embedded fonts, bookmarks, etc.

C. Include the appropriate copyright clearance code notice on the bottom of the first page of your paper:

a) For papers in which all authors are employed by the US government, the copyright notice is: U.S. Government work not protected by U.S. copyright

b) For papers in which all authors are employed by a Crown government (UK, Canada, and Australia), the copyright notice is: 978-1-6654-7100-8/22/$31.00 ©2023 Crown

c) For papers in which all authors are employed by the European Union, the copyright notice is: 978-1-6654-7100-8/22/$31.00©2023 European Union

d) For all other papers, the copyright notice is: 978-1-6654-7100-8/22/$31.00 ©2023 IEEE

Latex users belonging to the category in (d) can directly add the following lines just before \begin(document) for the copyright notice to show up:

\IEEEoverridecommandlockouts \IEEEpubid{\makebox[\columnwidth]{ 978-1-6654-7100-8/22/\$31.00~\copyright~2023~IEEE \hfill} \hspace{\columnsep}\makebox[\columnwidth]{ }}

MSWord users can use: Insert Text box, insert the appropriate copyright notice in the textbox, and place the box (without border) at the bottom left on the first page.

III. VALIDATE THE PDF OF YOUR CAMERA-READY PAPER FOR XPLORE COMPATIBILITY.

Check the compatibility of your PDF file in the IEEE PDFeXpress application using the link:

https://ieee-pdf-express.org/Account/Login

This tool will also let you create IEEE Xplore-compliant PDFs.

A. First-time users should register themselves with the conference ID: 55242X

Continue to enter information as prompted. An Online confirmation will be displayed and an email confirmation will be sent verifying your account setup.

B. Previous users of PDF eXpress need to follow the above steps but should enter the same password that was used for previous conferences. Verify that your contact information is valid.

The most common error found in files that fail to meet IEEE Xplore® compliance requirements is the failure to embed all fonts.

IV. SUBMIT YOUR PAPER

Submit the IEEE Xplore®-compatible PDF file of your camera-ready paper (with the same name generated from PDF eXpress) on EDAS using the following link:

https://edas.info/index.php?c=29624

A. Go to the EDAS entry of your paper.

B. Locate the Paper Options row and click on the upload button under the final manuscript column.

C. Browse for the IEEE Xplore®-compatible PDF file of your camera-ready paper and click on the Upload button.

==========================

TRANSFER OF COPYRIGHTS

In order to have your paper published in the conference proceedings and in the IEEE Xplore, you need to transfer a copyright form for each paper that you have accepted at the conference.

Following the recent policies of the IEEE concerning the transfer of ownership rights of intellectual property to IEEE, we have enabled the submission of copyright forms using the electronic copyright form (eCF) tool of the IEEE. Follow the instructions below to submit your copyright form using the eCF. Make sure that you mention a complete list of authors' names in the copyright form.

Log in to the EDAS using the link: https://edas.info/index.php?c=29624

A. Go to the EDAS entry of your paper.

B. Locate the Paper Options row and click on the record copyright form under the copyright column.

C. Click on the IEEE Copyright Submission button and follow the instructions, then finally submit the copyright form of your paper.

The above information will be updated soon on the official conference website:

https://www.ieeesilcon.in/

REGISTRATION POLICY FOR IEEE SILCON-2023:


  • In order to publish your paper in the IEEE SILCON-2023 Conference Proceedings and to be eligible for publication in IEEE Xplore, at least one author of an accepted paper is required to register for the conference.

  • The paper must be presented by an author of that paper at the conference unless the TPC Chair grants permission in advance of the event for a substitute presenter, who is qualified both to present and answer questions.

  • Non-refundable registration fees must be paid prior to uploading the final IEEE formatted, publication-ready version of the paper.

  • A student author/co-author of accepted papers at IEEE SILCON-2023 is eligible to present his/her only ONE accepted paper on the concessional rate of IEEE/non-IEEE student member registration.

  • An author/co-author (student or non-student) of accepted papers at IEEE SILCON-2023 is eligible to present his/her maximum of TWO accepted papers on full IEEE/non-IEEE member registration.

  • Receipt of the Registration Fee will be provided to the registered authors only.
